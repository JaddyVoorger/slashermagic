3. Manifesto


Over time, the ecosystem of Magic: The Gathering has evolved into an increasingly protected environment.

Sleeves, playmats, and cases have turned cards into objects that are almost untouchable.

The Slasher format proposes the opposite: encouraging risk, improvisation, and the freedom to play without fear of actually using the cards.

Extremely optimized decks may exist, but they do not represent the primary spirit of the format.

4. Foundations


4.1. What it is

Slasher is a casual Magic: The Gathering format built on the premise that cards exist to be played. The experience of the match is more important than the perfect preservation of the cards.

For this reason, the format prohibits the use of accessories that protect cards during gameplay, such as sleeves or playmats. The natural wear of the cards is part of the format’s experience.

In the Slasher format, at the end of a match, the winner chooses one card from the defeated opponent’s deck.

This rule has a fundamental purpose: to naturally keep the average value of cards used in the format low.

Since any card used in a match may be lost, players tend to avoid using extremely expensive or difficult-to-replace cards. This risk creates a natural economic mechanism that encourages the use of more accessible cards.

In other words, the risk of loss itself functions as a balancing system for the format.

If a card becomes too powerful or dominant, its demand tends to increase, raising its price. As the card’s value rises, the risk of using it in Slasher matches also increases, discouraging its use.

This cycle creates an environment where the power of cards and their economic value tend to balance naturally.

4.2. Who it was designed for

The Slasher format was created for players who want to experience Magic: The Gathering in a more accessible way, with less dependence on financial investment.

It is especially intended for:

- Casual players: People who want to play in a relaxed way without worrying about preserving cards.

- New players: Players who are just starting the game and have small collections.

- Players with unused cards: The Slasher format creates an environment where rarely used cards can regain strategic relevance.

- Local communities: Groups of friends or stores that want to create a more inclusive play environment.

4.3. What problem it solves

As Magic: The Gathering has grown, many formats have begun to require increasingly higher investments in order to compete on equal terms.

This creates challenges such as:

- Financial barriers to entry;

- Inequality between decks based on economic value.

Slasher seeks to mitigate these problems by naturally encouraging the use of more accessible cards.

5. Philosophy


5.1. Foundation

The Slasher format is based on the idea that cards are game tools. By removing physical protection, the format encourages players to explore accessible cards and reduces the incentive to use highly valuable ones.

The Slasher format values:

- Creativity;

- Experimentation;

- Accessibility;

- Social interaction.

More than a set of rules, the Slasher format proposes a different way of relating to the game.

5.2. What the format encourages

The Slasher format encourages:

- Creativity in deckbuilding;

- Use of accessible cards;

- Experimentation and strategic diversity;

- Player interaction;

5.3. What the format discourages

The Slasher format naturally discourages:

- The use of high-value cards;

- Dependence on expensive staples;

- Excessive focus on collection preservation.

5.4. Match trophies

In the Slasher format, cards won in matches can become true gameplay trophies.

By winning a match and choosing a card from the opponent, the player takes with them not only a game piece, but also a memory of the match that took place.

Over time, decks may begin to contain cards won in different matches, creating unique stories for each player.

5.5. Card history

Some players may choose to record the origin of cards by writing information directly on the card they won, such as:

- When the card was won;

- Which player it came from;

- In which match it was obtained.

This practice is not mandatory, but it represents the spirit of the format well.

6. Rules


6.1. Deck structure

- Minimum of 60 cards;

- Up to 4 copies of the same card, except basic lands;

- Optional sideboard of up to 15 cards.

6.2. Match structure

Matches are played as best-of-three games (Bo3), following the traditional rules of Magic: The Gathering.

After the match ends, the defeated player must allow the winner to examine their deck and sideboard in order to choose the won card.

The winner chooses the card freely. The opponent cannot prevent the choice.

It is not allowed to:

- Hide cards;

- Remove cards from the deck before the choice;

- Reorganize the deck to make the choice more difficult.

6.3. Valid cards

All cards from all Magic: The Gathering sets are valid in the Slasher format, except those listed in section 6.4 Invalid Cards.

6.4. Invalid cards

The following are not allowed:

- Proxies: The goal of the format is to keep cards with low values in decks; proxies could discourage this practice.

- Gold-bordered cards: These are generally staple cards with more accessible values, which would influence deck construction.

- Foil cards: Forbidden because they may create unintentional visual markings in the deck.

Cards that are scribbled on, bent, or altered in a way that makes them easily identifiable while in the deck are also not allowed.

6.5. Valid accessories

Accessories that do not physically protect cards during gameplay are allowed, such as:

- Dice;

- Markers;

- Life counters;

- Tokens;

- Life tracking apps.

- Deckboxes or containers may be used for storage outside of matches.

6.6. Invalid accessories

Accessories that protect cards during gameplay are not allowed:

- Sleeves;

- Double sleeves;

- Shields;

- Playmats.

The only protection allowed is the use of gloves by players, if they wish.

7. Ban list


The Slasher format currently does not have a list of banned cards.

The philosophy of the format is based on the principle that its own rules act as a natural balancing mechanism.

If a card becomes too strong in the format:

- Its popularity increases;

- Its demand grows;

- Its market value tends to rise.

As the card's value increases, players tend to avoid using it, since it may be lost or damaged during matches.

In this way, the card market itself can act as a form of metagame self-regulation.

If necessary, a ban list may be introduced in the future.

9. Governance


9.1. Who decides changes

The Slasher format does not have a formal committee. Changes are conducted by the maintainer of this document.

9.2. Review frequency

There is no fixed review schedule. Changes are made as necessary.

9.3. How to send suggestions

9.4. How changes are announced

All changes to the Slasher format are published through updates to this document.
